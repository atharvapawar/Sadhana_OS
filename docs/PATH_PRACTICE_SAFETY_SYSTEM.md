# Phase 8 — Path & Practice Safety System

This phase upgrades the existing **Path & Practice** section into a safety-gated practice system without adding new main pages or changing the 18-stage river.

## Purpose

Sadhana OS should show that advanced yogic technologies exist, while clearly protecting the user from unsafe self-experimentation.

The practice architecture now separates:

1. Beginner-Safe Foundation
2. Steady Practitioner
3. Teacher-Guided Depth
4. Do Not DIY Protected Zone

## Core Rule

Knowledge may be visible. Practice depth is gated by readiness.

Every advanced topic follows this pattern:

- This exists in the tradition.
- This is its purpose.
- This is why it is powerful.
- This is why it is protected.
- This is the prerequisite.
- This is the beginner-safe alternative.

## Practice Lanes

### Lane I — Beginner-Safe Foundation

For new, inconsistent, restless, or rebuilding practitioners.

Focus:

- Right View reflection
- Yama/Niyama awareness
- Gentle asana
- Natural breath observation
- Soft mantra or silence
- Karma Yoga in ordinary duties

Avoid:

- Forced kumbhaka
- Aggressive breath ratios
- Kundalini activation attempts
- Strong cleansing
- Advanced mudra/bandha

### Lane II — Steady Practitioner

For users with consistent practice and stable daily life.

Focus:

- Longer sitting
- Safe breath balancing without strain
- Mantra japa
- Pratyahara through sensory discipline
- Karma Yoga integration

Avoid:

- Many intense methods at once
- Experience chasing
- Spiritual comparison
- Confusing numbness with meditation

### Lane III — Teacher-Guided Depth

For mature practitioners with a qualified teacher or lineage context.

Focus:

- Specific pranayama sequencing
- Bandha/mudra progression
- Mantra depth
- Subtle concentration
- Integration through humility and service

Avoid:

- DIY advanced kriya
- Kundalini triggering
- Practicing through warning signs
- Treating experience as proof of realization

### Protected Zone — Do Not DIY

This is not a practice lane. It is a boundary.

Protected topics include:

- Forced kumbhaka and strong ratios
- Advanced bandha-mudra combinations
- Kundalini/chakra activation attempts
- Strong shatkarma and extreme fasting

## Safety Gates

The page now includes three gates:

1. Breath Gate
2. Energy Gate
3. Meditation Gate

Each gate separates green, yellow, and red signals so the user understands when to continue, slow down, or stop intensifying practice.

## Non-Negotiables

- No advanced DIY instructions.
- No forced breath retention guidance.
- No activation language.
- No promise of siddhi, kundalini awakening, or guaranteed enlightenment.
- Every powerful method must point back to readiness, humility, teacher guidance, and grounding.
